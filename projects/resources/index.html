                AI Self Study Resources

                Published: 2020-02-10

                  Updated: 2020-02-17

                # Step 1: The Landscape

                The first step in the process is to get an idea of what you are about to get into. These podcasts, videos and other high level resources talk about how AI, ML and DL are being applied to the world, how people working on those fields think and how they got their start.

                • The Age of AI: This YouTube original series with Robert Downey Jr. takes viewers on a journey of all the ways AI is being applied to the world today and the amazing work with real impact that people are doing.

                • 80,000 Hours Podcast: The 80,000 Hours podcast focuses on helping people make the biggest impact possible with the careers. One of their core identified tracks is A.I. and several episodes feature people working in A.I. such as OpenAI employees. A few episodes of relevance are:

                  • #01 – Miles Brundage on the world's desperate need for AI strategists and policy experts
                  • #03 – Dr Dario Amodei on OpenAI and how AI will change the world for good and ill
                  • #23 – How to actually become an AI alignment researcher, according to Dr Jan Leike
                  • #28 – Dr Owen Cotton-Barratt on why scientists should need insurance, PhD strategy & what if AI progresses fast
                  • #31 – Prof Allan Dafoe on defusing the political and economic risks posed by existing AI capabilities
                  • #44 – Dr Paul Christiano on how OpenAI is developing real solutions to the 'AI alignment problem'...
                  • #54 – Askell, Brundage & Clark from OpenAI on publication norms, malicious uses of AI, & general-purpose learning...
                  • #58 – Pushmeet Kohli of DeepMind on designing robust & reliable AI systems and how to succeed in AI
                  • #69 – Jeffrey Ding on China, its AI dream, and what we get wrong about both

                • Artificial Intelligence Podcast by Lex Fridman: Lex Fridman is a well known in the world of Artificial Intelligence and lectures often at MIT. His podcast interviews many well known researchers and personalities in AI. All episodes are worth watching but here are some of the notable ones for this list:

                  • Max Tegmark: Life 3.0
                  • Elon Musk: Tesla Autopilot
                  • Jeremy Howard: fast.ai Deep Learning Courses and Research
                  • Michio Kaku: Future of Humans, Aliens, Space Travel & Physics
                  • Elon Musk: Neuralink, AI, Autopilot, and the Pale Blue Dot
                  • Sebastian Thrun: Flying Cars, Autonomous Vehicles, and Education

                # Step 2: Books

                These following books are not highly technical books. Rather, they focus on how AI was born, how it's growing today and how it'll impact us in 5 years, 50 years and even in 5 billion years.

                • Life 3.0: Being Human in the Age of Artificial Intelligence
                • AI Superpowers: China, Silicon Valley, and the New World Order
                • Superintelligence: Paths, Dangers, Strategies
                • The Fourth Age: Smart Robots, Conscious Computers, and the Future of Humanity
                • Artificial Intelligence: Confronting the Revolution
                • The Big Nine: How the Tech Titans and Their Thinking Machines Could Warp Humanity
                • Human + Machine: Reimagining Work in the Age of AI

                # Step 3: Introduction to Deep Learning

                The first step in technical understanding of deep learning. This is a short list - the focus is to gain the minimum understanding required to start doing stuff with AI. The FastAI course is specifically designed to get people moving with deep learning with little code and a great deal of understanding.

                • FastAI: Practical Deep Learning for Coders, v3

                # Step 4: Build Stuff

                This is a self directed step. The only way to truly gain an understanding of machine learning, the workflow, the challenges and solutions is to start by picking something to build and working on a small project to completion. This step can't be skipped, no number of videos or books will show you what building an ML solution actually entails (and will open you eyes to the limitations and possibilities for ML). The FastAI course has several suggestions and ideas for where to get started but in the end, work on something that interests you and bring AI in to that process

                # Step 5: Deep Dive into Deep Learning

                The next step is to move up from understanding how to use libraries like FastAI to understanding how they work and how you can modified and improve them. Again, FastAI is on this list for the Part 2 of their course. Additionally, several tutorial series like Python Programming and Deep Lizard are great choices for deeper understanding.

                • FastAI: Deep Learning from the Foundations
                • DeepLizard: Neural Network Programming - Deep Learning with PyTorch
                • Python Programming: Deep Learning and Neural Networks with Python and PyTorch

                # Step 7: The Journey Continues

                This list will only help you get started in Artificial Intelligence. The field is changing everyday with new concepts, ideas and research coming to the field every day. The process is one of continual learning. Some suggestions for staying on top of new developments:

                • Two Minute Papers
                • 80,000 Hours Podcast
                • Reddit ML and AI Subreddits
                • Researcher App
                • Upcoming yearly FastAI courses
